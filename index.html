Marrakesh: The city of wonders

Ever heard of the pretty city? If not, let me tell you about its beauty

Between tradition and modernity, Marrakech is the promise of unequalled sensations. Strolling the Jemaa El-Fna square and the souks with their shimmering colours and oriental smells, the red city offers you a complete change of scenery. Horse-drawn carriage rides, sunny terraces, travelling artists and other day and night activities will give a tates of wishful comeback.

Djemaa El Fna

Talking about Marrakash without mentioning Djemaa El Fna is a crime! It's by far the wildest part of the country. Having sellers from different kinds, different talents and shows that please all the kinds, such as:

  • Halqa: Traditional StandUp comedy
  • Taktouka: Historic Greeting Way
  • Animals Shows: Especially with monkeys and snakes

Koutoubia Gardens

The Koutoubia Gardens sit adjacent to the Koutoubia Mosque, and it's by far the easiest space to escape the chaos of North Africa’s most iconic square, Djemaa El Fna. A little peace and quiet lies only a short walk across the road from the main entrance along the square's western side.

Menara Gardens

West of Marrakesh at the gates to the High Atlas Mountains, the Menara Gardens were also established by the Almohads. Like Agdal, the Menara Gardens have a large basin that was used to irrigate the surrounding groves and orchards using a sophisticated network of aqueducts.
